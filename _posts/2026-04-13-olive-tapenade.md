I come from the Koura district, a region known for its olive tree fields. So obviously I woke up today graving some tapenade, a dish that my ancestors never heard of. Coming from the land of olive trees, I went to the supermarket and bought some imported olives, grandpa would be proud. Turned these olives into a puree, that apparently people who hate Eunade, call it Tape Eunade. 

## Ingredients

* 1 big handful of pitted black olives
* 1 small clove of garlic
* salt
* Black pepper 
* 1 tablespoon Zaatar (not mixed with olive oil)
* 1 teaspoon pomegranate molasses
* 1 sundried tomato
* Sundried Tomato Oil (or Olive Oil)


## Instructions

1. On a cutting board, place the above Ingredients
2. Get a really sharp knife and start knifing untill they're all mixed together into a chunky-paste like consistency
3. Thank you for your attention, girl
4. Get some labneh, and put it on top of your labneh and serve yourself 
5. You can eat it as a dip with some toasted bread or chips
