## Ingredients

* Ricotta cheese, but it has to be homemade
* 1 big lemon, or more, or less, or same
* 400g Fusilli Pasta
* 3 small zucchinis 
* 1 garlic clove (not too big, not too small)
* Salt 
* Black Pepper 
* Olive Oil, the good kind

### Now for THE PASTA

1. Invite Alex over and don't tell her that you don't pay for the elevator subscription and that she needs to go 8 floors up
2. Don't let her touch the food, because you know, I only, belong in the kitchen. 
3. I personally don't wash the zucchini because we need to save water and it's gonna get cooked anw. So don't wash your zucchinis
4. In a stainless steel bowl you great gurl, grate the zucchinis, salt them and let them sit for 10 to 15 minutes so they throw their water 
5. After 15 minutes, as I mentioned in step 3 of my amazing scientific article, put the zucchini in a cheese cloth, or in an old Hanin fanella you have, and squeeze the hell out of them, to remove all excess water. 
6. Olive oil a pan, throw in your zucchini and shallow fry them (i like them a bit firm, but to each their taste). 
7. Once they're 92.54% cooked, grate the garlic clove, and continue cooking till your reach 100%. We have a late stage garlic dish, because we like the garlic taste. 
8. Sometime in between cooking the zucchinis, cook your pasta, al dente of coursi 
9. Now get a bigger stainless steel bowl, mix you homemade ricotta, it's actually nicolemade, why do I keep saying homemade? 
10. Ok so mix my ricotta, with salt, black pepper (a like the kick of it), some olive oil, 1 whole lemon, and the zest of the lemon. 
12. Add one cup of your pasta water and mix well
13. Add the cooked zucchinis and mix well 
14. Add the cooked pasta and mix well 
15. Sometimes I feel I repeat myself, I think I need to mix well 
16. Serve
